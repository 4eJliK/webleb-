What is SEO? (Search Engine Optimization)

October 5, 2022
SEO Website Ranking Search Engine Optimization

SEO stands for “search engine optimization.” It means the process of improving your site to increase its visibility when people search for products or services related to your business on Google, Bing, and other search engines. The better your website is visible in search results, the more likely you are to attract customers to your business.

How does it work?
Crawling is the process by which search engines like Google and Bing collect information about websites, going from site to site and indexing them. You can think of the index as a giant library where a librarian can pull up a book (or a web page) to help you find exactly what you're looking for.

In order to determine the order in which pages should appear in the search results for a given query, algorithms analyze pages in the index, taking into account hundreds of ranking factors or signals. Organic search ranking isn't paid, so SEO experts have to work hard to get higher rankings organically.

Why is SEO important?
SEO is important because it keeps the search results fair. It reduces the possibility of manipulating them as much as possible, ensuring that the sites appearing for each search are there because they deserve to be there. A good search engine ranking is correlated with hard work and a website that is appealing to visitors, so if your site meets those criteria, you're more likely to show up.Your site's top ranking in search engine results indicates to searchers that it is a credible business, and users trust search engines. When your site ranks high in search engine results pages, you'll get more clicks and visitors. In addition, SEO improves the user experience, increasing the likelihood of repeat purchases.

Important SEO Factors:

  • High-quality content
  • Create a Secure Website (HTTPS)
  • Optimize Your Page Speed
  • Enhance Page Experience
  • Create A Good Internal Link Structure
  • Earn Relevant & Authoritative Links
  • Keyword Usage
  • Make Your Site Mobile-First
